## Motion should confirm structure

Motion is useful when it tells the user what changed, what belongs together, or
where attention should land next. It becomes noise when it tries to entertain.

At Sonicverse we bias toward:

- short distances
- soft easing
- reduced-motion respect
- meaningful grouping

<Callout title="Rule of thumb" tone="info">
  If animation cannot explain itself in product terms, it usually does not belong in
  the system.
</Callout>

## Where subtle motion helps most

### Entry and reveal

Small reveal animations help sections feel sequenced instead of dumped onto the page.

### Hover feedback

Cards and actions can lift slightly to confirm interactivity without shouting.

### Dense interfaces

When pages contain structured data, motion can help users maintain context as
elements load or reflow.

## The premium effect

Restrained motion often feels more technical and more expensive than aggressive
animation. It suggests confidence in the structure, not dependence on spectacle.
